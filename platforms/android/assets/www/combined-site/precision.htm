Sun Changes through the Seasons

The Sun always Rises in the East and Sets in the West.

But the time and angle in the sky of the Sun changes through the seasons, so SunDial shadows will change slightly too. Daylight Savings Time effects the measurements as well.

As the Sun-light circles the Earth (actually the Earth rotates) every 24 hours past each Longitude, the timezone of each Longitude changes. Every sphere, including Earth, is 360°, so there are different timezones every "15 minutes" of Longitude: 24(hours) ÷ 360° = 15 minutes. A timezone is just an offset from Greenwich, UK's UTC Time, so that Noon in Greenwich looks like Noon where you live +-timezone-offset. You can describe a Longitude in Degrees and Minutes or as a decimal (ex. 42.5 Longitude = 42:30 Longitude.
